About Injera

Injera is a sourdough flatbread from Ethiopia and Eritrea, typically made from teff flour. It serves as both a plate and utensil in traditional Ethiopian meals, with stews and salads placed on top.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups teff flour
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste

Cooking Instructions

Click to expand
  1. Mix teff flour, all-purpose flour, and water in a bowl to form a smooth batter.
  2. Add yeast, cover, and let sit at room temperature for 1-2 days to ferment.
  3. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and pour a thin layer of batter.
  4. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges lift, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Remove from the skillet and repeat with the remaining batter.
