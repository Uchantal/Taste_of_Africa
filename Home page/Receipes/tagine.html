About Tagine

Tagine is a traditional North African dish that originates from Morocco. It is a slow-cooked stew made with meat (often lamb or chicken), vegetables, dried fruits, and a variety of spices like saffron, cumin, and cinnamon. The name "Tagine" also refers to the unique clay pot in which it is cooked.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 chicken thighs (or lamb, beef, or vegetables)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp saffron (optional)
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup dried apricots, chopped
  • 1/4 cup slivered almonds
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)

Cooking Instructions

Click to expand
  1. In a large Tagine pot or heavy skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add chicken (or meat) and brown on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add onions and garlic, cooking until softened.
  4. Stir in cumin, cinnamon, ginger, and saffron (if using), cooking for another minute.
  5. Add chicken broth, apricots, and almonds. Bring to a simmer.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat for about 45 minutes to an hour, until the meat is tender.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with couscous or flatbread.
