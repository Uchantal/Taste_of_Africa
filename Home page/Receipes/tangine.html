Moroccan Tagine Recipe

About Tagine

Tagine is a traditional North African dish that is named after the special pot used to cook it. It is a slow-cooked stew, often made with lamb, chicken, vegetables, dried fruits, and spices. It is usually served with couscous or bread.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg lamb or chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 cup dried apricots, chopped
  • 1/2 cup almonds, toasted
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Couscous or bread for serving

Instructions

Step-by-Step Instructions
  1. Heat olive oil in a tagine or large pot over medium heat. Brown the lamb or chicken pieces on all sides.
  2. Remove the meat and set aside. In the same pot, saut√© the onion and garlic until soft and fragrant.
  3. Add the spices (cumin, cinnamon, turmeric) and stir for 1-2 minutes until aromatic.
  4. Add the tomatoes, broth, dried apricots, and meat back into the pot. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Cover and cook on low heat for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the flavors have melded together.
  6. Garnish with toasted almonds before serving with couscous or bread.
